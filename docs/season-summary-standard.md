# Pragmatic Season Summary Standard

A proposal states intent, completed and partial work, missed or changed priorities, evidence about what helped or hindered, and concrete adjustments. It distinguishes recorded facts from observations and uncertainty; separates execution, planning, scope, and external constraints; avoids invented causation, accomplishments, diagnoses, shame, and generic encouragement; and remains constructive and future-positive without overstating evidence. It never changes future plans automatically.
