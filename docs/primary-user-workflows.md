# Primary-User Workflows

These workflows describe intent and information flow, not screens, routes, persistence, or exact interaction design.

## Workflow A: Season Lifecycle

| Phase | User intent | Required / optional information | System behavior | Historical preservation | Open questions | Release |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Create a season | Establish a bounded planning context. | **Required:** name, dates. **Optional:** description. | Create a draft season without assuming a standard length. | Preserve the season's original identity; later date-edit history is undecided. | Fixed length or user-selected dates? | Initial |
| 2. Define season intent | State the period's emphasis and guide tradeoffs. | **Required:** intent statement. **Optional:** supporting context. | Associate intent with the season and make it available during planning/review. | Completed seasons retain their intent. | Can intent change after activation, and must edits be versioned? | Initial |
| 3. Add goals | Limit attention to meaningful priorities. | **Required:** title, season. **Optional:** description. | Associate every goal with one season; support a lifecycle status. | Removed/completed goals must not vanish from completed-season history. | Maximum active goals? | Initial |
| 4. Define measurable outcomes | Describe observable success for each goal. | **Required:** description and goal. **Optional:** type, target, unit. | Keep outcomes distinct from work and allow conservative result types. | Retain recorded progress and final results. | How are outcome types and scores calculated? | Initial |
| 5. Add execution structures | Translate goals into useful intermediate work. | **Required:** title/type and relevant parent where applicable. **Optional:** dates/context. | Allow milestones, habits, recurring actions, or projects only as useful; do not require all. | Completion must not erase a source structure. | Habit versus recurring action? Project versus goal? How is work generated? | Initial, limited |
| 6. Review season dashboard | Check scope, balance, and readiness. | **Required:** season plan. **Optional:** warnings/summaries. | Compose planning information without owning it; support priority/scope review. | No new historical record is inherently required. | Which readiness signals and limits matter? | Initial, limited |
| 7. Enter daily loop | Turn the plan into repeated action. | **Required:** current date and relevant work. **Optional:** priorities/context. | Hand off to Workflow B while retaining links to planning sources. | Dated execution records remain available after source changes. | Stored, generated, or hybrid daily tasks? | Initial |
| 8. Complete weekly scorecard | Inspect structured weekly progress. | **Required:** season and week. **Optional:** measures/summary. | Summarize authoritative records without rewriting them. | Retain each submitted scorecard and its period. | Calendar versus season weeks? Overall score formula? | Initial, limited |
| 9. Write weekly reflection | Interpret progress, friction, and lessons. | **Required:** season/week and reflection. **Optional:** prompts. | Present relevant scorecard and daily history as inputs. | Do not silently overwrite prior content; editing/version policy is undecided. | Which prompts and editing rules? | Initial |
| 10. Adjust execution | Respond to learning while keeping goals meaningful. | **Required:** intended adjustment. **Optional:** rationale. | Change future plans/statuses without rewriting completed actions. | Preserve prior schedules, completions, and reflections. | Which changes require explicit history? | Initial, basic |
| 11. Complete season review | Assess results and experience at period end. | **Required:** season and review summary. **Optional:** per-goal lessons/results. | Present season goals, outcomes, activity, and weekly reflection context. | Preserve the completed snapshot and review. | Can reviews be reopened or versioned? | Initial |
| 12. Capture carry-forward insights | Select lessons worth retaining. | **Required:** insight and historical source. **Optional:** category. | Link each insight to a review/reflection; do not create next-season work automatically. | Source relationship remains traceable. | Manual, suggested, or mixed selection? | Initial |
| 13. Start next season with context | Plan with prior lessons visible. | **Required:** new season details. **Optional:** selected prior insights. | Make context available without copying or mutating old records. | Prior seasons remain independently accessible. | Which context appears by default? | Initial, limited |

## Workflow B: Daily Mobile Execution

| Phase | User intent | Required / optional information | System behavior | Historical preservation | Mobile considerations | Open questions | Release |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Open primary daily view | Understand what matters immediately. | **Required:** effective calendar date. **Optional:** short context. | Compose current daily information; the view owns no records. | Opening the view need not create history. | Fast load, clear first viewport, touch-first hierarchy. | Name, route, and default landing behavior? | Initial |
| 2. Review priorities | Identify the few most important items. | **Required:** today's relevant priorities. **Optional:** source context. | Distinguish priorities from lower-priority work without changing domain ownership. | Priority-change history requirements are undecided. | Priorities should be visually distinct and scannable. | How are priorities selected or ranked? | Initial |
| 3. Review due work | See tasks, habits, and recurring actions relevant today. | **Required:** title, status, date/source where known. **Optional:** goal context. | Present distinct concepts consistently without assuming one generation model. | Preserve source links where present. | Avoid dense planning detail; provide large targets and concise labels. | Should generated items have stable task identities? | Initial |
| 4. Act on items | Complete, defer, reschedule, or dismiss supported work. | **Required:** selected action/item. **Optional:** new date or note. | Apply only explicit actions; basic deferral/rescheduling may be deliberately limited. | Completion records the occurrence; deferral must not silently erase prior scheduling history. | Common actions need minimal navigation and clear feedback. | Which actions ship first? Does unfinished work roll forward? | Initial, limited |
| 5. Add manual task | Capture unplanned work without leaving the loop. | **Required:** title/date. **Optional:** source or priority. | If supported, allow a standalone source without forcing a goal. | Retain its completion and schedule history like other tasks. | Entry should be brief and usable one-handed. | Supported initially? Must it belong to a season/goal? | Undecided for initial |
| 6. Record daily check-in | Capture brief context about the day. | **Required:** date only; entry is skippable. **Optional:** short note. | Keep the initial contract minimal and avoid a required long form. | Associate one record with a calendar date; editing/version rules remain open. | Complete in seconds; progressive disclosure if structured fields arrive later. | One per date? Which mood/energy/sleep/focus/blocker fields? | Initial, limited |
| 7. Preserve daily history | Trust that completed work and context remain. | **Required:** dated completion/check-in data. **Optional:** timestamps/notes. | Keep authoritative daily records independent from current view composition. | Do not delete records through rollover, source completion, or season transition. | History need not crowd the daily surface. | Retention and historical editing rules? | Initial |
| 8. Feed reflection and insights | Use activity as evidence for learning. | **Required:** dated authoritative records. **Optional:** aggregates. | Supply records to weekly reflection; future Insights may derive summaries without mutation. | Reflections link to stable periods and do not rewrite source records. | Reflection may use a denser layout; daily capture stays lightweight. | Which summaries appear first? | Initial for reflection; advanced insights later |

## Connection between the workflows

Planning supplies context and possible sources for daily execution. Execution produces dated facts and lightweight check-in context. Weekly scorecards summarize those facts; weekly reflections interpret them; season reviews combine planning intent, outcomes, daily history, and weekly learning. Carry-forward insights select historical lessons for future planning without rewriting the season that produced them.
